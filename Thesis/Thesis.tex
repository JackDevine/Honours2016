\section*{Abstract} % This section will not appear in the table of contents due to the star (\section*)
As Brownian particles diffuse over a potential landscape, they draw energy from their environment in the form of heat. We account for these interactions by coupling the Smoluchowski equation to a modified version of the heat equation yielding two coupled non-linear partial differential equations. We show that the interactions can induce temperature gradients that significantly alter the dynamical behavior of the particle. We obtain theoretical steady state solutions to the equations of motion and employ numerical techniques to explore the dynamical properties of the system. We find the Kramers' rate for our modified view of Brownian dynamics and we make concrete observations of Brownian cooling and the reverse Landauer blowtorch effect. We show that these phenomena can significantly alter the motion of a particle on a tilted periodic potential, in particular we show that for particular sets of parameters, the movement of the particle can be brought to a stand-still. This has direct consequences to biology in the context of molecular motors as well as the dynamics of colloidal particles.
\end{center}
